The Proposal

Abby has told me about her fond memories of going to California when she was younger. Specifically, she would tell me about her trips to the redwood national forests while she was out there and how seeing the redwood trees was always so magical to her. She always made those trips sound so fun and I had never seen the redwoods so I promised that one day we would go out there and she would have to show me around.

Well the opportunity came knocking when a good friend of mine, who lived in California, said he was getting married and asked me to be a groomsman. We decided to take a week so after my friend’s wedding we could see the sites she use to see when she was a child. One of those stops was the Sequoia National Park.

The day could not have been better. The sky looked like a painting of a perfect summer day, sun shining brightly through a stark blue sky with the occasional white fluffy cloud lazily floating along.

We started walking around the park with a loose destination to see General Sherman, one of the largest trees in the world. As we explored around, Abby was excitedly recounting her adventures coming to this area as a child but it was hard for me to concentrate as I knew, without a doubt, that this was the right time to ask, but I still needed the right place.

We saw the giant sequoia, General Sherman, and then began exploring the crisscrossing paths that traversed the park at the base of the tree. That’s when I decided we needed to get off the main path and find a special place just for us. I stepped off the designated path and after hiking a short way into the forest we found an outcropping of rocks that offered a little nook. I looked around at where we were, looked at Abby, and everything felt just right.

That’s when I took a knee and asked her to marry me.
